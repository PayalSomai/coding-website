WTF...What The Feminism

feminism

/noun/

the belief and aim that women should have the same rights and opportunities as men


This is your Oxford Dictionary definition of feminism and to be honest, I don’t think it does the word justice. I say this because feminism is so much more! Feminism is about creating equality amongst people regardless of who they are or where they come from. A term you might have heard before is ‘intersectional feminism’ and as scary as it sounds, it just means that it includes race, gender, sex, age, identity, welath and socio-economic status.


It’s important to understand that when you talk about feminism, you have to talk about intersectional feminism as well, otherwise you’re not really helping the world. You see, intersectional feminism takes all the factors I mentioned above (and many more) into consideration and it shows how feminism is such a broad and complex topic to talk about because it means different things to different people.


To put into simpler words, a man’s experience of feminism would be drastically different compared to a woman’s experience of feminism. And if we bring race into the equation, a white woman’s experience of feminism would be drastically different from a Woman of Colour’s (WOC) experience of feminism. And if we bring sexual orientation in, a heterosexual white woman’s experience of feminism would be different compared to a queer WOC’s experience of feminism. And this can go on for any factor of intersectional feminism.


Now, this doesn’t mean that just because your experience of feminism is different, you can’t talk about it. It just means that you need to be mindful because no two people will have the same story. And that’s where I come in!


This website will help show feminism through the ages, from female scientists who you didn’t know help make groundbreaking discoveries to poets and authors who stopped following the status-quo. It’ll give you the resources you need to be part of the feminst movement and make you feel like you can make a difference.
